# Executive Summary

## Objective
Analyze the relationship between Bitcoin Fear & Greed sentiment and Hyperliquid trader performance.

## Methodology
- Cleaned and validated both datasets.
- Standardized timestamps and merged data at daily level.
- Engineered Daily PnL, Win Rate, Drawdown Proxy, Trade Frequency, Average Trade Size and Daily Volume.
- Compared trader performance and behaviour across sentiment categories.
- Performed trader segmentation based on Win Rate.

## Key Insights

### Performance
Fear periods were associated with higher average daily profitability (5,328.82) and lower average drawdown than Greed periods.

### Behaviour
Trading activity peaked during Extreme Fear (133.75 trades/day), indicating increased trading activity during uncertain market conditions.

### Trader Segmentation
Consistent Traders achieved the highest average daily PnL (12,985.27), the highest average win rate (85.62%), and lower drawdown than other segments.

## Strategy Recommendations

1. Apply sentiment-adaptive risk management by reducing position size during Greed periods.
2. Prioritize high-quality trade selection over increasing trading frequency.

## Conclusion
Market sentiment is associated with meaningful differences in trader behaviour and performance. Combining sentiment awareness with disciplined execution may improve trading outcomes.
